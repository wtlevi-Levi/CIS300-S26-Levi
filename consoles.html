Nintendo's Home Consoles

Overview

Although Nintendo is most well known for their console lineup, prior to their entry into the home gaming market they created arcade games. After other large gaming companies like Atari were facing a decline in sales, Nintendo saw an opportunity to enter the market with their first home console: the Nintendo Entertainment System.

A New Direction

After the Wii U failed to meet sales expectations, Nintendo knew that they had to truly impress audiences with their next console in order to win back their fans. The Wii U's successor, the Nintendo Switch, was advertised as a home console that would also allow you to play games either portably or on your television. After creating a handheld system with the power of a home console, the Nintendo Switch became the company's best selling console to date. With the release of the upgraded Nintendo Switch 2, it is clear that Nintendo hopes to emulate their success with a more powerful system that recreates everything that was great about the original and more.

What's Next?

Although the original Nintendo Switch is still receiving new games and updates, it is clear that support for the console is winding down fast and that the console will receive fewer major releases as time goes on. Nintendo is rapidly shifting their focus towards the Switch 2, which will likely be supported until at least 2031. It is unsure what the company will do for their next console or when it will even release, but if the Switch 2 is successful enough, it is likely that Nintendo will continue to create hybrid gaming consoles that can be played at home or on the go.
