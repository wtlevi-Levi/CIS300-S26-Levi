Power In Your Pocket

Overview

Nintendo's lineup of handheld consoles have historically been some of their most popular and successful devices released by the company. Even when home consoles like the Wii U underperformed, Nintendo always managed to find success in their dedicated portable machines. Many of Nintendo's classic titles from the 80's and 90's received portable releases that allowed fans to take their favorite games with them anywhere they pleased. After the success of the hybrid Nintendo Switch that acts as both handheld and home console, it is unsure if Nintendo will ever make another exclusively handheld gaming device again.

Dual Screen Innovation

With the release of the DS in 2005, Nintendo made a bizarre device with a fascinating gimmick: gaming on two screens at once. Additionally, the bottom screen had touch functionality, and some games would require users to tap or move a stylus pen across the screen to complete certain actions. Although the release of the DS was initially a huge gamble for the company, it ended up becoming Nintendo's most successful handheld console, and their second most successful device after the Nintendo Switch.

Upgrades & Alterations

Because all of Nintendo's handheld consoles were successful, Nintendo was more willing to create upgrades and different versions of their devices. Nintendo's first variation of the Game Boy was the Game Boy Pocket, a smaller and more portable version of the original device. The Game Boy Color famously allowed all original Game Boy games to be played with a color filter, as opposed to the original single color screens. The DSi was an important upgrade over the original DS as it had a built-in camera that allowed users to take digital pictures with their device as well. Finally, both the DS and 3DS received "XL" versions, which featured larger screens and a bigger device overall.
